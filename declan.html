Rome

I traveled to Rome in early January and absolutely loved the experience. The city is gorgeous and holds so much history. I would recommend four to five days to see everything and maximize your time there. This is definitely a trip you can do with friends or a significant other. Between the food and landmarks, it is a must-see destination. I can definitely see myself returning in the future.

Where to Stay

I went with my girlfriend and three of our friends. When we arrived, the first thing we did was check into our Airbnb. Through all of my travels, I have concluded that Airbnb is the best way to find somewhere to stay. Especially when traveling in groups, hostels can be dangerous and are not very different in price. We stayed in two different Airbnbs. The first was a little farther from downtown, and the second was closer to Trastevere. Trastevere was definitely the place to be, and although it cost more, we saved money on taxis, which ultimately made it a similar overall price.

The Food

The food throughout the city is incredible, and anywhere you go will likely be a good choice. There are also many coffee shops that we popped into randomly for an espresso and a quick bite of fresh prosciutto or other snacks. The highlight is definitely the pasta and gelato. For the best pasta, we went to Osteria da Fortunata, which was recommended as having some of the best pasta in the city. We did not go anywhere specific for pizza, but we found plenty of great options in coffee shops and local spots.

What to See

My biggest recommendation is the Colosseum. The history is felt as soon as you walk in, and there is nothing else like it in the world. Other great spots are the Trevi Fountain, Piazza Navona, the Pantheon, and the Roman Forum. If you are interested in art, then go to the Borghese Gallery, which has some of the most astonishing art I have ever seen. And although it is technically separate from Rome, Vatican City is also a must-see. Regardless of religion, it has an aura that makes you think deeply and reflect on yourself.

Paris

I traveled to Paris for Valentines Day with my girlfriend, and the atmosphere of the City of Love was obvious everywhere we went. Even though I know Paris would feel special on almost any weekend, going during that time made the trip even more memorable. Overall, this was my favorite place I have traveled to. I was there for four days and still felt like I barely saw a quarter of the city. The food, architecture, and attractions were unlike anything else, and I can definitely see myself returning in the future.

Where to Stay

We used Airbnb to find a place in the Eleventh Arrondissement. It ended up being more like staying in someones spare bedroom than renting an entire apartment, but it was actually a great experience. The homeowner was there during our stay, and we ended up spending time talking with him and getting to know him. If that idea sounds intimidating, I would still recommend giving it a chance. The Eleventh was a great area to stay in, especially because we were right next to public transportation. Getting around the city was very easy, and staying slightly outside the main tourist areas helped us save money without feeling far from anything important.

The Food

If you do not walk into every pastry shop you see and try something new, then you are doing Paris wrong. Everywhere I looked, there was something that made me stop in my tracks. A few must-visit places are Cosi for a sandwich, Krugen Gardette for a crepe, and Junk for a great smash burger. Bar De Vie is also worth stopping at for some of the best cocktails I have ever had. What stood out most was not just the food itself, but also the atmosphere and service. Every stop felt authentic, which made the experience even better.

What to See

Be ready to walk a lot and be impressed the entire time. We averaged around 20,000 steps a day and still struggled to see everything before we left. The most obvious landmark is the Eiffel Tower, and I highly recommend paying to go to the top. The view lets you appreciate the scale of the city, and doing it at the end of your trip makes it even cooler because you can point out places you already visited. The Louvre area was also amazing, even just from the outside, because there is so much to see around it. Another major highlight was the Arc de Triomphe. As a fan of the Tour de France, seeing it in person felt especially meaningful. Paris has everything: sightseeing, shopping, food, and museums. Try to fit in as much as you can, because a weekend really is not enough time.

Barcelona

I traveled to Barcelona to visit a friend during the first weekend of my spring break. Overall, I had a very fun experience, and the city had a great energy during the day. It was also nice to get a break from Dublin and enjoy warmer weather. At the same time, Barcelona felt much more influenced by American study abroad students than some of the other cities I visited, which changed the atmosphere a bit. While I could definitely see the beauty of the city, the nightlife made me a little more cautious. During the time I traveled there, there were reports of theft, a train crash, and other unsettling stories, so it felt like a place where being aware of your surroundings really mattered.

Where to Stay

Once again, we used Airbnb and stayed in a place that fit eight people. It had a kitchen, living room, two bathrooms, and a bed for each of us, so it worked well for a larger group. We did stay a little farther from downtown than I would have preferred, which made getting around a bit harder. Public transportation helped, but we still spent a lot of time walking and taking trains. We also ended up using quite a few Ubers and taxis, since they were relatively affordable. For an offseason trip, the location worked out fine, but during the summer I would probably recommend staying closer to the beach or the main parts of the city.

The Food

Barcelona has some great seafood and fresh meats, and one of the best parts of eating there is how affordable it can be. We mostly did tapas dinners, which usually meant ordering things like mussels, scallops, sardines, and plenty of patatas bravas. I also enjoyed the amount of wine and beer available everywhere we went. The trip made me more interested in Spanish wineries and how the flavors differ from other regions. If you are going to Barcelona for the food, expect a lot of seafood and tapas, because that is really what stands out most.

What to See

The most famous landmark in Barcelona is the Sagrada Familia. I still remember stepping off the subway and seeing it standing there for the first time. It was especially cool to visit in 2026 because the monument is finally nearing completion. It is definitely one of the busiest and most tourist-heavy parts of the city, so you have to be prepared for crowds, scammers, and a little chaos. We also did a great hike to watch the sunset and see the city from above, which ended up being one of my favorite parts of the trip. Overall, Barcelona is a place to enjoy tapas, warm weather, shopping, and major sights, but it is also a city where you need to stay alert, especially at night. I would recommend going with a group of friends rather than doing it as a solo trip.
