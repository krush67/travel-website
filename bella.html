Copenhagen

I visited Copenhagen the last weekend of January and absolutely loved the experience. The city is charming, with colorful streets, canals, and a unique blend of modern design and rich history. I would recommend spending four to five days to explore everything and really soak in the atmosphere. This is a trip perfect for friends or a significant other. Between the cozy cafes, world-class food, and iconic landmarks like Tivoli Gardens and Nyhavn, Copenhagen is a must-see destination. I can definitely see myself returning someday.

Where to Stay

When traveling to Copenhagen, I would highly recommend staying in Vesterbro. This neighborhood has such a vibrant and trendy energy, full of cool cafes, bars, and restaurants, making it a fun place to explore day or night. Just nearby is Kodbyen, also known as the Meatpacking District, which is a super lively area with art galleries, nightlife, and unique food spots its one of the citys most exciting hubs. Vesterbros location is perfect because its close to the city center, so getting to major attractions like Tivoli Gardens, Nyhavn, and Stroget is quick and easy, but it still feels like youre experiencing authentic local life. The combination of trendy vibes, accessibility, and fun streets makes this area one of the best places to stay in Copenhagen.

The Food

Copenhagen is a foodies dream and truly lives up to its reputation for incredible cuisine. We started our mornings at Buka Bakery, where grabbing a fresh, flaky croissant is an absolute must. For dinner we went to Cleo and tried their tasting menu! The food was phenomenal and the trendy, modern atmosphere made the experience even more memorable. The city is full of spots that combine creativity with quality, from casual bakeries to high-end restaurants, making it a culinary destination you wont forget. For drinks, Jojos is a standout with its cocktails on tap, and the surrounding bars in Vesterbro and nearby Kodbyen are just as incredible, each with its own unique vibe, making it easy to enjoy a lively night out or a relaxed evening in one of Copenhagens most vibrant neighborhoods.

What to See

When visiting Copenhagen, there are several sights you absolutely cant miss. The Little Mermaid statue is iconic, and while small, its a must see for first time visitors. The Nyhavn canal area is perfect for wandering along colorful buildings, grabbing a drink, or taking a boat tour through the citys waterways. The Botanical Gardens offer a peaceful escape with beautiful plants, glasshouses, and walking paths that showcase Denmarks natural beauty. Other notable spots include Tivoli Gardens, especially if you enjoy amusement parks with a historic charm, and Rosenborg Castle, where you can see royal treasures and explore the stunning gardens. Copenhagen blends history, charm, and modern design, making each of these sights a memorable part of your trip.

Milan

I visited Milan in early February and loved the experience. The city is stylish and full of energy, blending fashion, history, and culture. I got to visit my best friend Anya, who studies at Bocconi University, which made the trip extra special. My favorite part was a day trip to Lake Como. The serene waters and charming towns were absolutely breathtaking. We also attended the Olympics opening ceremony, which was unforgettable. Between the incredible food, iconic landmarks like the Duomo, and the beauty of Lake Como, Milan is a city I would definitely love to return to.

Where to Stay

If youre visiting Milan, Id recommend staying near the Navigli canal area. This neighborhood is lively, trendy, and full of character, with plenty of cafes, bars, and restaurants right along the canals. Its perfect for evening strolls, aperitivo, or hopping between nightlife spots, and its still close enough to reach landmarks like the Duomo and Brera in just a short walk or tram ride. Staying here really gives you the best mix of Milanese culture, energy, and convenience.

The Food

Milan is a dream for food lovers, especially if you enjoy hearty, flavorful meals. We had an amazing sandwich at Panino Ignorante, and brunch at Sisu was absolutely incredible fresh, creative, and very aesthetic. Along the Navigli canal, we stopped at Bella Riva, since it was named after me of course, for a relaxed meal with a great view. Compared to Rome, we quickly realized Milan is very much a meat city, so if you love steak, cured meats, and rich dishes, this is the place for you. Between the casual spots, trendy cafes, and canal side restaurants, Milans food scene is both delicious and full of character.

What to See

In Milan, theres so much to see that youll want a few days to explore. Be sure to visit the Duomo, Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II, and the artistic Brera district for a mix of history, shopping, and culture. The Navigli canals are also a must for wandering and enjoying the vibrant atmosphere. I would also recommend taking a day trip from Milan, as there are so many incredible places easily accessible by train, such as Lake Como, Bergamo, and Pavia. These nearby towns offer stunning scenery, historic architecture, and a change of pace from the city, making them perfect for a full day adventure.

Dubrovnik

Last but not least, I visited Dubrovnik, and it quickly became my favorite spot. The scenery is absolutely breathtaking, with crystal-clear waters, red-tiled rooftops, and historic city walls that feel straight out of a movie. The culture and history are everywhere, from the charming Old Town streets to the stunning churches and fortresses. The lifestyle in Dubrovnik is so relaxed yet vibrant, with lively cafes, seaside promenades, and a love for fresh seafood and local wine. On top of all that, the city is surprisingly affordable, making it easy to enjoy everything without breaking the bank. Walking through the city, you can really feel the mix of history, beauty, and everyday life, making it a place that is as captivating as it is unforgettable.

Where to Stay

If youre visiting Dubrovnik, you can definitely stay in the Old Town to be in the heart of the action, but its also great to stay outside the city and discover nearby towns. We stayed in Mlini and loved exploring this charming seaside town with its relaxed culture and beautiful waterfront. Although it looks far on the map, nothing is more than a 10 minute drive, and Ubers are very cheap, so getting to Dubrovnik or other nearby spots was quick and easy. Staying outside the city gave us a chance to enjoy a quieter pace while still being close to all the action.

The Food

Dubrovnik is full of delicious food, but there are a couple of must try dishes. The one thing you absolutely have to try is Panino Romano, a sandwich place that serves fresh, flavorful sandwiches perfect for a quick meal while exploring. Another highlight is the black risotto, a local seafood dish made with squid ink that is rich, savory, and uniquely Dubrovnik. Between casual spots like Panino Romano and traditional restaurants along the coast, the citys food scene is fresh, affordable, and full of local flavor.

What to See

When in Dubrovnik, there are several sights you absolutely must see. The Old Town is a must, with its historic walls, narrow cobblestone streets, and stunning architecture. Walking along the city walls gives incredible views of the red tiled rooftops and the sparkling Adriatic Sea. You also have to visit Buza Bar, a cliffside bar where you can enjoy drinks right above the ocean. Its views and unique setting are unforgettable. The fortresses and churches throughout the city showcase Dubrovniks rich history, while the Stradun, the main street, is perfect for wandering and soaking in the local atmosphere. For a bit of nature and scenery, taking a short trip to nearby beaches or viewpoints adds an extra layer to the experience.
