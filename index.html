Our Homebase

Dublin became our shared home away from home and the center of so many of our favorite memories abroad. As a group, we got to experience the city in different ways, from trying new restaurants and pubs to spending afternoons exploring neighborhoods, parks, and coastal towns nearby. What makes Dublin special is that it feels both lively and comfortable at the same time. It has the energy of a city, but it still feels approachable and easy to settle into. Over time, we each found our own favorite parts of it, and together those experiences shaped our view of Dublin as much more than just the place where we studied.

Food We Kept Coming Back For

Dublin has a strong food scene with a wide range of cuisines, but one of the most recognizable is classic pub grub. Pub grub is made up of warm, comforting foods such as stews, burgers, bangers and mash, and fish and chips. You can find it at many of Dublin’s best pubs, but be aware that kitchens often close fairly early, so it can be hard to get them later at night. Beyond pub food, Dublin also does a great job with other cuisines, especially Chinese, Indian, and fast food. For breakfast, we would recommend either Volume for a bagel or Brother Hubbard for a classic Irish breakfast. For lunch, any good pub with a kitchen is usually a safe choice, but we also really like Honest to Goodness for a chicken Caesar wrap. Other great food spots include Xi’an Street Food, and not just for the spice bag. It is worth trying other items on the menu too. If you are looking for good pizza, go to Bambino or Doom Slice. They are very different styles, but both are excellent in their own way.

Pubs That Defined the Atmosphere

Dublin’s pub scene really changes depending on the area you are in. We found ourselves grouping it into four main spots: Temple Bar, Dame Lane, Camden Street, and Baggot Street. Temple Bar is definitely a must-see at least once. It is more expensive than other areas, so it is not the kind of place we kept going back to, but it does have some of the best live music in the city. If you go, good stops are The Temple Bar, The Old Storehouse, and The Auld Dubliner. Dame Lane is one of the best areas because it is central to everything and has a great variety of pubs, each with a different feel. Some of our favorites there were Harry Lemon, J.T. Pim’s, and Hogan’s. Camden Street is also a great area, but it feels more geared toward college students and a younger crowd. Good spots there include Ryan’s, The Bleeding Horse, and Flannery’s. Finally, Baggot Street had some of the biggest and most beautiful pubs we found in Dublin. While the main names there are O’Donoghue’s, Toners, and Doheny & Nesbitt, those three alone offer great music, fun atmospheres, and excellent pints. Some other notable pubs we loved outside of those main areas were McCafferty’s, Mother Reilly’s, Kodiak, and Kehoe’s.

Favorite Daytime Activities

If you are visiting Dublin and have a free day with nothing planned, this is what we would recommend. One of our favorite easy day trips is Blackrock, where you can walk around, browse shops, and enjoy the coast. On weekends, there is also a market there with several incredible food stands and restaurants, making it a great place to stop and try something new. Another bonus is that the DART station is right there, which makes it easy to continue on to another great day trip, Howth. Howth is a charming fishing town with excellent seafood, beautiful coastal views, and a great hike around the peninsula for anyone who enjoys being outdoors. Another great day trip is Glendalough, which has one of the best hikes near Dublin. It is definitely better for someone with more hiking experience, but it is worth the effort. If you want to stay in the city, other good options are walking through St. Stephen’s Green, exploring Grafton Street, or spending the day pub-hopping through the city center. My biggest recommendation for how to spend a day in Dublin is to go on a trad music tour around the city. Trad music is one of the most unique parts of Irish culture and is something everyone should experience. Some notable trad pubs are The Cobblestone, McNeill’s, and The Celt.

Sites Worth Seeing

When you are in Dublin, there are several stops you have to make. First is St. Stephen’s Green, which is gorgeous and a great way to start your time in the city. Next, check out Trinity College and the Book of Kells. From there, make your way through Grafton Street, stopping in shops, cafes, or pubs along the way. Two museums we have visited and would highly recommend are the Guinness Storehouse and the Jameson Distillery. Both are great experiences where you get to learn, interact, and enjoy drinks throughout the visit.
