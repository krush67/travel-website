London

I traveled to London in late January with my girlfriend, who was also studying abroad, and it ended up being a very enjoyable weekend trip. It was the first trip I went on during the semester, so it really set the bar high. London feels like one of those cities where you could stay busy for days and still leave feeling like you barely scratched the surface. There is always another neighborhood, landmark, market, or restaurant to check out. I would absolutely recommend it, especially for a weekend trip, because there is such a good mix of major sights and just wandering around the city.

Where to Stay

We stayed in a cheap hotel in South Kensington, which I thought was a really nice area to stay in. It was quiet, clean, and felt like a very comfortable neighborhood to come back to at the end of the day. For the part of London we were in, the location was really good, although it is definitely farther from some of the more eastern parts of the city. Still, I liked being based there, and if you stay in South Kensington I would definitely recommend taking time to walk around and enjoy the area itself instead of only using it as a place to sleep.

The Food

The food in London is very diverse, which is one of the best parts of visiting, but if you want a more authentic experience I think you have to go to a pub at least once. The downside is that pubs can be expensive, and sometimes they are almost the same price as going to a more formal restaurant. That said, I still think they are worth it for the atmosphere. I really liked fish and chips and would recommend ordering that at most pubs. I would also recommend Nando's, which is a fast casual spot that serves grilled chicken with peri sauce and is a good option if you want something easy that still feels pretty classic for a London trip.

What to See

There are so many major sights in London that it is hard to narrow them down, but I would definitely make time for Tower Bridge, the London Eye, Westminster Abbey, Buckingham Palace, and Borough Market. Those are some of the places that really make the city feel like London. Borough Market was one of my favorite spots because it gives you something to do beyond just checking off landmarks. In general, London is a great city for walking and just letting the day unfold naturally, because even when you are moving between the big tourist sites it still feels like there is always something else worth stopping for.

Rome

I went to Rome over Valentine's Day weekend to visit my girlfriend, who was studying there, and it was one of those cities that feels huge the entire time you are in it. Rome is definitely one of the major capitals in Europe and there is a lot to do and see, which makes it a must-see if you are abroad. At the same time, it is also very touristy, so that shapes the experience a lot. Even with that, I still thought it was a really fun trip and a place that is worth visiting because of how much history and energy the city has. It feels like one of those places you almost have to see for yourself at least once.

Where to Stay

I stayed in Trastevere, which I thought was a really good area. It had a lot of cafes, plenty going on, and more personality than just being near the major tourist sites. There was also a lot of nightlife around there, so it felt lively without being overwhelming. If I went back, I would probably stay in the same area again because it had a good balance of atmosphere and convenience. It made the trip feel a little more local, even in a city that is so heavily built around tourism.

The Food

The food was good overall, but I honestly was not as blown away by the pasta as some people told me I would be. That might just be because I am not a huge pasta person, or maybe I ended up in a few places that were more average than exceptional, but it was not life changing in the way people sometimes describe Rome. I still enjoyed eating there, and there are obviously a lot of solid options throughout the city. I just think the food was a little overhyped compared to the expectations people set for me before the trip.

What to See

I would definitely recommend seeing the Colosseum, the Trevi Fountain, Vatican City, and the Monument to Victor Emmanuel II. Those are the kinds of places that make the trip feel worth it. One thing I would especially recommend is using Lime scooters or bikes to get around the city. The cobblestone roads can be a little treacherous, so it is not always the smoothest ride, but it is a great way to cover more ground and see a lot in a short amount of time. Since the weather is usually good enough for it, I thought that was one of the best parts of getting around Rome.

Mallorca

I went to Mallorca with my girlfriend for a weekend in early April, and it was a really nice change of pace from city trips. We stayed around Palma and Palmanova, and the whole trip felt much more relaxed and beach-focused than sightseeing-heavy. It was around 65 degrees Fahrenheit, so warm enough to enjoy being outside without it feeling too hot. Mallorca had a very easygoing feel to it and was a great place for a short getaway. For me, it was less about trying to do as much as possible and more about slowing down and enjoying the setting.

Where to Stay

We stayed in Palmanova, which felt very family friendly and was a really easy place to spend a weekend. There were nice beaches within close walking distance, and there was a lot of good food nearby right along the beach. I would recommend staying in that area, especially for a shorter trip, because it is simple and comfortable. At the same time, if you are in Mallorca for longer than a few days, I would definitely recommend seeing other parts of the island too. There are clearly a lot of different areas with different personalities, and it would be worth exploring more of them. Since we did not have a rental car, we were pretty limited to our area unless we wanted to pay for expensive taxis.

The Food

The food was really good, and one of the best parts was how easy it was to find solid places without overplanning anything. We mostly just walked down the beach and picked restaurants that looked the best to us, and none of them let us down. That made the whole trip feel very easy and low pressure. I would recommend getting a mojito or another Spanish cocktail and just enjoying the scenery. For this trip, the food was less about finding one famous place and more about sitting outside, having a good meal, and taking in the setting.

What to See

For us, the point of Mallorca was more about beaches and relaxation than trying to fit in a long sightseeing list. Other than the cathedral in Palma, there was not one major landmark that stood out to me as a must-see over everything else. I would be more likely to recommend specific beaches, calas, and coastal areas than monuments or museums. It is the kind of place where the setting is really the main attraction. If you go there expecting a slower and more scenic trip, I think that is the best way to enjoy it.
